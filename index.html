Food Waste and Unsustainable Diets = Inefficient Use of Resources

American food habits are unsustainable. We not only consume a lot of meat and dairy (which requires more land and water to produce and releases more emissions), but we also waste a lot of food. This food waste translates to energy and resources that are lost, and if the food ends up in landfill, it can produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to global warming.

Nutrition and environmental interests are, in fact, compatible. Making sustainable choices–like eating a plant-based diet and choosing organic–can benefit your health by lowering the risk of heart disease, high cholesterol, and cancer. By eating green, you can also support local farmers and save money!
